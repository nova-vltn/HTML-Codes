About NASA

Leading the world in space exploration and overseeing America's civil space program is the
National Aeronautics and Space Administration.

Just under 18,000 civil servants make up the agency's varied workforce, and it collaborates
with several academic institutions, commercial and international partners, and U.S. contractors to
explore, discover, and develop knowledge for the good of humanity.

Worldwide organizations such as NASA have done extensive research on the complexities of stars in the universe.

For more than 50 years, NASA has been breaking barriers to achieve the seemingly impossible.
At its 20 centers and facilities across the country – and the only National Laboratory in space –
NASA studies Earth, including its climate, our Sun, and our solar system and beyond.

        A Record of Ingenuity
        NASA has explored the uncharted since its founding in 1958 by pushing the frontiers of science and technology.

        NASA advanced the work already underway on robotic and human spaceflight when it opened for operation on October 1, 1958, and during the following 65 years, 
        it has persisted in pushing the frontiers of space exploration and aviation.

On May 3, 2021, Sen. Bill Nelson took the oath of office as the 14th Administrator of NASA, charged with implementing the policies and plans of the Biden-Harris administration.
